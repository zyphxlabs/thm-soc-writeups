# SOC Role in Blue Team

## Security Hierarchy
Every company has different security priorities.
Law firms care about privacy. Hospitals care about patient safety.
Factories care about availability.

CEO focuses on business — hires a CISO to handle security.
CISO oversees all security departments.

## Security Departments
- Red Team — offensive security, ethical hackers, pentesters
- GRC Team — policies and compliance like PCI DSS
- Blue Team — defensive security, SOC analysts, incident responders

## Blue Team Structure
Blue Team monitors attacks and responds to them quickly.
Can have 3 to 50 members depending on company size.

SOC — first line of defense, handles most alerts and attacks
- L1 Analyst — triages alerts, passes complex cases to L2
- L2 Analyst — investigates advanced attacks
- Engineer — configures security tools like EDR and SIEM
- Manager — manages the whole SOC team

CIRT — called when SOC cannot handle the incident alone
- Handles major breaches without depending on tools
- Examples: JPCERT, Mandiant, AWS CIRT

Specialized Roles:
- Digital Forensics Analyst — uncovers hidden threats in disk and memory
- Threat Intelligence Analyst — researches emerging threat groups
- AppSec Engineer — secures software development lifecycle
- AI Researcher — studies AI threats and defenses

## Internal SOC vs MSSP
Internal SOC — you protect one company, calm pace, few tools but know them deeply.
MSSP — you protect many clients, high pressure, many tools, see more incidents faster.

Both are valid starting points. MSSP gives more exposure to real attacks early on.

## SOC Career Path
Start as L1 → gain experience → move to L2 or specialize.
Options after L1: L2 Analyst, SOC Engineer, CIRT, Threat Intel, or CISO path.

Four tips for SOC analysts:
- Learn from every single alert
- Think like an attacker
- Verify everything before acting
- Get involved in real incidents whenever possible
