# Humans as Attack Vectors — Writeup


## Why Humans Are Targeted

Humans are the easiest target because they already have access to everything, emails, systems, databases, so instead of trying to break through technical stuff for days an attacker just tricks a person and gets in, simple as that

## Social Engineering

This is when you trick a person instead of hacking a machine, for it to work it has to look real so the person trusts it and it has to make them feel something like fear or urgency so they just do it without thinking.

## Attack Types

Phishing is fake emails that have bad links inside, 3.4 billion of these are sent every single day. malware downloads are fake apps that look totally normal but have something harmful hidden in them. deepfakes are when an attacker uses AI to make a fake video call while pretending to be a real person, a finance worker got fooled like this and ended up sending 25 million dollars to an attacker

- impersonation — Acting like someone else basically to copy someone else identity
- USB drops — the infected usb drive with malicious code left so some body uses them and get infected
- insider threats — someone who is already inside the company  with bad intentions and causes the damage 
- fake job offers — they pretend to offer a job to get info or make someone click something

## Mitigation vs Detection

Mitigation is stopping the attack before it reaches you, detection is catching what already had gone wrong, you need both because no system is perfect and some weakness will always present there is always a room for improvement

- anti phishing tool that blocks bad emails before they land
- antivirus and EDR on every single device
- train employees so they know how to spot something suspicious
- run fake phishing tests to see if the training is actually working

## Key Takeaway

Most attacks do not start from breaking some system, they start from a normal person making a mistake, so a good SOC analyst has to understand people not just look at alerts all day, keeping people safe is just as important as keeping systems safe
